Publications

Evaluation of the Growth, Adaptation, and Ecosystem Services of Potentially Introduced Urban Tree Species under Drought Stress

Key words: Urban forestry, drought stress, ecosystem services, exotic tree species, root system adaptation, evapotranspiration, climate resilience, Tilia species, urban greening, Guangzhou

Under rapid urbanization and increasing drought risk in Guangzhou, this study evaluated the drought adaptability of two exotic tree species (Tilia cordata and Tilia tomentosa) in comparison with the native species Tilia miqueliana through a drought-simulation experiment. Results showed that Tilia miqueliana and Tilia tomentosa exhibited stronger drought-resistance strategies, particularly through root development, evapotranspiration, and cooling capacity, while Tilia cordata experienced significant declines in growth and ecosystem services.

DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-023-30782-x
